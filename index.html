Not Designed. Documented.

Wild Wild was not conceived in a boardroom. It was conceived in the field. On a family wildlife-photography trip to Rajaji National Park, the founder noticed something simple but telling about what people were wearing inside one of India's great forests.

What people wore fell into two camps. At one end, plain military-style camouflage — dull, generic, and out of place. At the other, at best, a good-quality but completely blank T-shirt: well-made, but saying nothing, representing nothing, and carrying none of the beauty or vastness of the Indian wildlife all around them.

There was simply no apparel made for the people who genuinely love these places — nothing that let them wear the wild they had come to witness.

Wild Wild was built on a single belief: every Indian species has a story worth wearing.

The Zone System

Wild Wild's product architecture is inspired by — and named after — the zoning framework of Indian national parks and tiger reserves. In a reserve, the Core Zone is the protected innermost zone where wildlife lives undisturbed. The Buffer Zone is the outer ring where human activity and the wild come closest to meeting.

Wild Wild borrows these names directly. Two zones. Two palettes. One brand.

Both zones share the same GOTS-certified organic cotton, the same 220+ GSM construction, the same Hahnemühle fine-art print insert. They differ only in palette — because palette is the only thing that should change between the forest and the city.

Core Zone

Safari & Everyday Wear

The innermost zone. Designed broadly — for wildlife professionals, photographers, safari travellers, and nature enthusiasts — and for anyone who simply wants beautiful, considered everyday wear.

The Core Zone deploys muted, low-contrast earth tones drawn from Indian forests, riverbeds, and dry-season landscapes: Chital Dust, Bear Mauve, Mugger Slate, Muntjac Umber, Jungle Canopy, Gaur Espresso, Snow Leopard Ash.

These colours keep your visual profile calm in the field — not because they hide you, but because they respect the wildlife around you. Worn anywhere. Informed by the field.

Buffer Zone

Lifestyle & Urban Streetwear

The outer zone. The lifestyle and urban streetwear line for Gen Z and millennial consumers. Identical organic-cotton construction, identical Hahnemühle prints — a radically different, high-saturation aesthetic.

The Buffer Zone's palette is drawn from India's most vivid wildlife: the Bengal tiger's gold, the Himalayan Monal's iridescent purple, the Kabini black panther's near-black, the Sarus crane's deep crimson. Panther Midnight, Bengal Ochre, Monal Violet, Sarus Crimson, Roller Lilac, Verditer Frost, Hornbill Ivory.

Bold, saturated, on-trend — and chosen precisely because that intensity belongs in the city, not the field.

Same quality. Same photography. Same price. Different world.

Defend the Habitat.

This is not a slogan borrowed from marketing. "Defend the Habitat" is what this brand does — on the P&L, in the field, with every sale. The tagline came before the brand name was finalised. It is the entire reason Wild Wild exists.

We do not engage in vague corporate philanthropy. Sustainability without immediate, verifiable action is compromised.

For our launch drop, Wild Wild is partnering with Wildlife SOS — one of India's largest and most credible wildlife protection organisations. Exactly 20% of net profits from every sale in this collection are transferred directly to them. They run the world's largest sloth bear rescue facilities, rescue and rehabilitate leopards from human-wildlife conflict zones, and provide lifetime care for rescued elephants. They ended the centuries-old dancing bear practice in India.

Their 80G certification means every rupee donated is tax-deductible and publicly documented. Our customers can see exactly where their contribution goes.

The 20% pledge holds for every drop. As Wild Wild grows, we will extend that same commitment to additional wildlife conservation organisations across India — each collection, a new partner. Same standard. Wider reach.

The Unboxing

Every Box a Gallery Segment.

Opening a Wild Wild shipment is an invitation to historical field documentation. Every order arrives in fully plastic-free, certified sustainable packaging.

Accompanying your garment is a gallery-grade Hahnemühle fine-art print — the original, uncropped field photograph that became your design, printed on FSC-certified Bamboo or Agave paper. On the reverse: the species' current survival metrics in the wild, and the exact camera telemetry from the moment of capture — focal length, shutter speed, lighting conditions.

Enclosed in a handmade Sage Green 300 GSM cotton-rag envelope from Sri Aurobindo Ashram, sealed by hand with a custom wood-mount stamp.

Every box tells the story. The garment is the photograph.
